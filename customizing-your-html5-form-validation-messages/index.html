Customizing your HTML5 form validation messages

Using the title-attribute for input-fields is, of course, recommended when you want to provide your visitors with some extra information besides the text in the <label>, something like ‘Please enter your full name’ would be a lot more useful and accessible than just asking for “Name”.

I noticed not many people are aware of this extra feature of the title-attribute, but if you use the title-attribute, this text will also appear in the error message when you use HTML5 form validation (which you do use, right?).

<label>
	Name
	<input required type="text" title="Please enter your full name">
</label>

Unfortunately, this doesn’t work in Firefox, but you can easily fix that with the non-standard x-moz-errormessage attribute:

<label>
	Name
	<input 	required 
		type="text" 
		title="Please enter your full name" 
		x-moz-errormessage="Please enter your full name">
</label>

This works across modern browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Opera and IE 10+), let’s hope Safari 8 joins the party this fall.
